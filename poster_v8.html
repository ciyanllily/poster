Research Master Thesis · Communication Science · Universiteit van Amsterdam · 2026

Let Language Resistant Strategies Speak For Us:
A Qualitative and Quantitative Analysis of the Nature of Chinese Female Stand-Up Comedians Shows

Yiran Zi  ·  15527700 Supervisor: Prof. A.C. (Anne) Kroon Data: Rock & Roast · Stand-up Comedy and Friends · The King of Stand-up Comedy (2017–2025)
Background
Why This Research?
  • Chinese female stand-up is growing fast — women's share on Rock & Roast rose from 7% → 42% (2017–2022).
  • In response to the muting status raised by Muted Group Theory (MGT), 3 resistance strategies were proposed (Houston & Kramarae, 1991) — but were they adequate for the current Chinese context?
  • All existing studies are small-scale and qualitative. Corpus-level patterns remain unknown.
Research questions & hypotheses

RQ1: To what extent do the resistance strategies originated from Muted Group Theory apply to Chinese female stand-up comedy, and what additional strategies, if any, emerge from this context?

H1: Among the six language-resistant strategies, naming the strategies of silencing is the most prevalent one.

H2: Among all pairwise combinations of the six language-resistant strategies, the co-occurrence of naming the strategies of silencing and irony/hyperbole is the most frequent.

Method
Two-Stage Design
Study 1 — Qualitative
23 transcripts · Thematic analysis in ATLAS.ti
→ Six-strategy framework developed
Cohen's κ = .659
↓
Study 2 — Computational
293 transcripts · gpt-oss-120b (zero-shot)
→ 469 gender-related segments coded
Validated: MICRO avg F1 (6 strategies) = .667
⚠ Rare strategies (n ≤ 6) validated with caution — low support limits reliable F1 estimation.
Framework
Six Resistant Language Strategies
From MGT (Houston & Kramarae, 1991) Emergent from data
Naming the Strategies of Silencing
Explicitly points out a gendered double standard or unequal consequence.
e.g. "don't dresses up" → lazy (women) vs. "chill & profound" (men)
Renaming Old Concepts
Reworks an existing label to expose its hidden gendered bias.
e.g. "mama's boy" — why blame the mother alone?
Creating Alternative Discourses
Coins new words or frames that make gendered experience nameable.
e.g. "ordinary yet confident men" (普信男)
Irony / Hyperbole
Accepts a gender norm on the surface, then exaggerates it to absurdity.
e.g. "Marry him!" chanted even after "he hits me"
Gender Subversion
Reverses gendered roles so the norm appears arbitrary.
e.g. women on TV praising each other's beer bellies
Collectivization
Channels individual observation into a collective appeal to women as a group.
e.g. "We strong women would rather die than fawn"

Key Finding — H2
The Most Frequent Strategy Pairing
Of all 15 possible pairings
Naming the Strategies of Silencing × Irony / Hyperbole
126 segments (26.9%)
2nd place: Naming × Gender Subversion — only 46 segments (9.8%)
Nearly 3× more frequent · McNemar exact p < .001

Robustness check: within naming-only segments (n = 436), irony/hyperbole remained the top co-occurring strategy (28.9% vs. 10.6% for gender subversion), confirming the affinity is not a base-rate artefact. H2 supported.

Discussion
Naming Dominates — Invention Remains Rare
Why do Renaming / Creating / Collectivization strategies remain so rare (2–4%)?
  • Cognitive demand: coining new vocabulary or voicing collective advocacy requires more than recognizing injustice — it demands building language or claims the culture does not yet normalize.
  • Institutional cost: feminist neologisms and direct women's advocacy are exactly what China's mainstream discourse treats with most caution — they can spread, stick, and trigger backlash & censorship. Yang Li coined 普信男 ("ordinary yet confident men") in 2020 → Intel ad removed within days; sustained online harassment followed.

Result: comedy excels at pointing at inequality, but rarely builds durable feminist vocabulary or direct advocacy.

Implications
Theoretical

Validates & extends MGT in a non-Western, commercial media context. The three classic strategies are not equally "alive" — diagnosis is ubiquitous, inventions remain rare.

Methodological

LLMs can scale interpretive feminist coding — while mapping the limits of automation. Rare categories still require human judgment.

Key Message
"Resistance widely operates in a safer way through a recurring formula: name the unfairness, then make it funny enough to circulate."
